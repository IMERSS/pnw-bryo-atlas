 <h2 id="distinguishing-features">Distinguishing Features</h2>

 Distantly-spaced squarish or triangular simple leaves crested in 3-7 large teeth mark this dainty species of moist canyons and forests on the open coast. Leafy plants are 2-3 mm wide, yellow to green, a are usually woven in and amongst other bryophytes, though they can also form loose tangles. Compared to other species within its genus, P. gracilis bears leaves that are relatively flat, not showing the prominent leaf folding more typical of <a href="/taxa/Plagiochila">Plagiochila</a> species.

 <h2 id="habitat">Habitat</h2>

 Moist to wet, often shady rock outcrops, boulders, cliffs, waterfall spray zones, decayed wood, and tree trunks (esp. yellow-cedar) in the lowland zone
