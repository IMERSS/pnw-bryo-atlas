 <h2 id="distinguishing-features">Distinguishing Features</h2>

 <a href="/taxa/Nardia">Nardia</a> insecta is a rarely seen arctic-alpine species notable distinct within its genus when in the fertile state on account of the combination of bilobed leaves, underleaves and antheridia and archegonia found on the same shoots ("paroicous"). The shoots are light green to red, horizontal to ascending and up to 3 cm long by roughly 1.5 mm wide.  The lateral leaves are shallowly bilobed to about 1/3rd their length with somewhat oblique attachment at their base across the width of the stem and underleaves are triangular or linear with red bases nest seen under the micrscope.
