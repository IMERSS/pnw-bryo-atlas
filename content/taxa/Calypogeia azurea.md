<h2 id="distinguishing-features">Distinguishing Features</h2>

A frequent species in the damp, dark and wet coastal coniferous forests in which it thrives, <a href="/taxa/Calypogeia">Calypogeia</a> azurea occurs as a small, creeping blue plant on decaying organic matter. The round lateral leaves overlap one another like shingles on a roof ("incubous"), while the underleaves are two lobed and divided to about half of their length. The characteristic blue hue can be subtle, but it is a manifestation of the aquamarine oil bodies that are clearly visible under the microscope.

<h2 id="similar-species">Similar species</h2>

With round, overlapping leaves, C. azurea could only be mistaken for other species in the genus <a href="/taxa/Calypogeia">Calypogeia</a>, most of which can be distinguished on the basis of the underleaf shape. Of these, only <a href="/taxa/Calypogeia">Calypogeia</a> mulleriana has an underleaf that is similarly divided to about half its length, but it lacks the blue hue of C. azurea.

<h2 id="habitat">Habitat</h2>

Decaying organic matter, especially wood in wet coastal forests.
