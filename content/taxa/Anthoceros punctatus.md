<h2 id="distinguishing-features">Distinguishing Features</h2>

Functionally a weed in the region, the infrequent occurence on A. punctatus on disturbed urban and agricultural soils is one of the only hints to its identification. It has a dissected and fluted deep green thallus with dark spots visible under the hand lens. The sporophytes when present are roughly 5cm long and darkened by the presence of black spores. 

<h2 id="similar-species">Similar species</h2>

Lacking bulbils and possessing dark, tall sporophytes, it is distinct from all other hornwort species in the region with the exception of <a href="/taxa/Anthoceros fusiformis">A. fusiformis</a>. The distinction is microscopic, with A. punctatus having spores that are pitted along their inside face, while <a href="/taxa/Anthoceros fusiformis">A. fusiformis</a> has spores that are spiny all over.
