 <h2 id="distinguishing-features">Distinguishing Features</h2>

 Asymmetrically 3-lobed leaves that are longer than wide and frequently crowned in clusters of bright-red gemmae are a good indicator of <a href="/taxa/Tritomaria">Tritomaria</a> exsecta. The leaf apices are acute, underleaves are absent and the gemmae, when examined under a microscope, are smooth. These plants typically rest horizontally against the tree trunks and decaying organic matter that they call home, with golden green shoots up to 2 cm long and 2 mm wide. 

 <h2 id="similar-species">Similar species</h2>

 See table for comparison to other species of <a href="/taxa/Tritomaria">Tritomaria</a> and <a href="/taxa/Trilophozia">Trilophozia</a>. This species can only be distingushed from the exceedingly similair <a href="/taxa/Tritomaria exsectiformis">T. exsectiformis</a> by examining the gemmae under the microscope. In that species, the gemmae are angular, while in T. exsecta they are smooth.
