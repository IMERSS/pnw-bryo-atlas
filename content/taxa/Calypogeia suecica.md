<h2 id="distinguishing-features">Distinguishing Features</h2>

A rarely noticed species of decaying wood in coniferous forests, C. suecica is a tiny (~ 1 mm wide) pale-green plant with two rows of round leaves that overlap in a shingle-like fashion. Close inspection with a hand lens should reveal an underleaf that is dissected to about half of its length and crowned by 2 or 4 somewhat sharp apices.

<h2 id="similar-species">Similar species</h2>

On decaying wood, C. suecica is most likely to be mistaken for <a href="/taxa/Calypogeia muelleriana">C. muelleriana</a> and <a href="/taxa/Calypogeia azurea">C. azurea</a>, both of which are bigger species with identical leaf arrangement on the tops of the shoot. <a href="/taxa/Calypogeia azurea">C. azurea</a> is easy to distinguish on account of its blue hue, but smaller plants of <a href="/taxa/Calypogeia muelleriana">C. muelleriana</a> will look very similar and may need microscopic examination to detect subtle differences in underleaf shape (more acute in C. suecica) and oil body form (segmented oil bodies are known in both species, but only C. suecica has them alongside simple oil bodies).
