<h2 id="distinguishing-features">Distinguishing Features</h2>

An uncommon to rare species, R. trichocarpa forms small rosettes (~1cm wide) on thin soils with open exposure. These rosettes are distinguished by an abundance of hairs present all around the margins and underside of the plant. 

<h2 id="similar-species">Similar species</h2>

<a href="/taxa/Riccia beyrichiana">Riccia beyrichiana</a> also has marginal outgrowths, but they are much shorter, much wider and much less numerous than those of R. trichocarpa. No other <a href="/taxa/Riccia">Riccia</a> species has the abundant fine hairs of R. trichocarpa. 

<h2 id="habitat">Habitat</h2>

Seasonally moist fine mineral soil, especially around steeper and rockier seepage sites and ephemeral pools. Uncommon to Rare, SW & SC BC. 

<h2 id="associated-species">Associated species</h2>

Gemmabryum barnesii, Pottia truncata, <a href="/taxa/Riccia sorocarpa">Riccia sorocarpa</a>, <a href="/taxa/Riccia beyrichiana">Riccia beyrichiana</a>, <a href="/taxa/Riccia">Riccia</a> nigrella, <a href="/taxa/Riccia cavernosa">Riccia cavernosa</a>, Pleuridium acuminatum, Philonotis capillaris
