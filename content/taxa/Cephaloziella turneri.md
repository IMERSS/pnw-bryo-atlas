<h2 id="distinguishing-features">Distinguishing Features</h2>

The "biggest" species in the genus (shoots to about .5 mm wide and 1 cm long), <a href="/taxa/Cephaloziella">Cephaloziella</a> turneri is an infrequent yellowish green-to-brown presence in summer dry rocky habitat, where it can be found on often-disturbed thin soil over balds and in crevices. The leaves are deeply bilobed (dissected more than 3/4 of the leaf length) and generously toothed from base to tip. Angular red gemmae are frequently present at the shoot tip. 

<h2 id="similar-species">Similar species</h2>

C. turneri can grow alongside <a href="/taxa/Cephaloziella divaricata">C. divaricata</a>, but leaves or that species are not toothed from top to bottom. The other toothy species of <a href="/taxa/Cephaloziella">Cephaloziella</a> occur in much wetter habitats (bogs for <a href="/taxa/Cephaloziella elachista">C. elachista</a> and <a href="/taxa/Cephaloziella spinigera">C. spinigera</a>, permanently moist rock ledges for <a href="/taxa/Cephaloziella phyllacantha">C. phyllacantha</a>).
