 <h2 id="distinguishing-features">Distinguishing Features</h2>

 Found on thin soil amongst other bryophytes on timberline rock outcrops, <a href="/taxa/Nardia">Nardia</a> geoscyphus forms mats of green to brown, suberect plants that are roughly 1 mm wide and up to 1 cm long. The shoots are composed of alternately-arranged elliptical leaves that are broader than long and unlobed (occasionally with two very shallow lobes). On the lower surface of the shoot, an unlobed underleaf should be visible, at least near the tips of the shoot (microscope may be neccessary here). This plant is "paroicous", meaning that the antheridia are found below the archegonia on the same shoots.

 <h2 id="habitat">Habitat</h2>

 Moist to dry humus, mineral soil, rock outcrops, cliffs, ledges, crevices, boulders, streambanks, and tundra in the upper montane, subalpine, and alpine zones
