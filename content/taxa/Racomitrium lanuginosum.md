<h2 id="distinguishing-features">Distinguishing Features</h2>

Exceedingly common in open habitats, this is a large grey-green carpet-forming moss. It is immediately recognizable by a grey frosted appearance when dry due to its long white-tipped, and coarsely toothed leaves borne on upright and variously branched shoots.

<h2 id="similar-species">Similar species</h2>

Other rock mosses in the <a href="/taxa/Grimmiaceae">Grimmiaceae</a> are similar but differ in the size and organization of the shoots. Members of the Genus Grimmia are usually smaller and grow in orderly cushions over rock. Other species of <a href="/taxa/Racomitrium">Racomitrium</a> (R. elongatum, R. ericoides, R. muticum, and R. canescens) are similar in growth form but the large serrated teeth on the leaf tips of R. lanuginosum separate it from all others.

<h2 id="habitat">Habitat</h2>

Dry, open, rocky habitats; also bog and tundra hummocks, and mineral soil from lowlands to alpine; common in coastal BC, infrequent elsewhere east of the Coast-Cascade Mts. [except absent from ne BC east of the Rocky Mountains].

<h2 id="associated-species">Associated species</h2>

Depends on habitat but in rocky areas commonly with <a href="/taxa/Racomitrium">Racomitrium</a> elongatum, R. heterostichum, <a href="/taxa/Polytrichum piliferum">Polytrichum piliferum</a>, <a href="/taxa/Polytrichum juniperinum">P. juniperinum</a>, P. commune, <a href="/taxa/Dicranum">Dicranum</a> howellii, D. scoparium; in bogs Sphagnum.
