<h2 id="distinguishing-features">Distinguishing Features</h2>

The rare (or at least overlooked in rare habitats) <a href="/taxa/Scapania">Scapania</a> gymnostomophila is endemic to wet limestone and marble boulders and bedrock around creeks, where if forms tiny obscure patches among other bryophytes of brown-green leafy shoots less than 2 mm wide and 1.5 cm long. The upper lobe is between 1/4-to-2/3rds the length of the lower lobe; both have an elongate aspect, smooth margins, rounded apices and are joined along a sharply folded, linear "keel". Under the microscope, the leaf cells posess one single large oil body, a feature that is unique to this species in the genus. 

<h2 id="similar-species">Similar species</h2>

In its riparian limestone habitat and with its elongate, smooth, unequal lobes, unlikely to be mistaken for any other species of <a href="/taxa/Scapania">Scapania</a> in the region. The presence of a single large oil body in the leaf cells is a microscopic character that is diagnostic for the species within the genus <a href="/taxa/Scapania">Scapania</a>. 

<h2 id="habitat">Habitat</h2>

Moist, calcareous mineral soil, rock outcrops, boulders, cliffs, and humus in the lowland and montane zones

<h2 id="associated-species">Associated species</h2>
